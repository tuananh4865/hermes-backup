# Viral App Principles

Framework from Blake Anderson for building apps that spread organically.

## Core Principles

### 1. Solve Big Problems

**Maslow's Hierarchy** — Focus on fundamental human needs:
- Survival (food, health)
- Sex / Reproduction
- Social Status

Products solving these problems are inherently more viral than niche tools.

**Examples:**
- Riz GPT → helps men get more girls (sex/dating)
- umax → face attractiveness rating (social status)
- Cal → calorie counting (health/fitness)

### 2. More Complicated = Less Viral

**3-Word Test** — Can you describe your app in 3 words?
- Riz GPT → "respond to girls"
- umax → "face analysis"
- Cal → "photo calorie counter"

**Why Simplicity Matters:**
- 3-second TikTok attention span
- Easier word-of-mouth
- Faster to demonstrate in content

### 3. "Did You Hear About This?"

Your app needs to be **remarkable** — people naturally tell friends about it.

**Shareable Moments Test:**
- Would someone bring it up at a party?
- Would they text their mom about it?
- Would they share it in a group chat?

**Shareable Content Types:**
- Screenshots (Riz GPT)
- Before/after transformations (umax)
- Showing off results (Cal meals/macros)

## Finding Viral Ideas

### Method 1: Scan Social Media

- Use Google Trends
- Explore Reddit communities
- Read comment sections

**Go outside your bubble** — create accounts for demographics you'd never normally engage with.

### Method 2: Research App Store

- Download every app in your space
- Watch their social media content
- Check SensorTower for downloads/revenue
- **Graveyard of failures with no successes?** → Avoid the space

### Method 3: Disconnect

Best ideas come in moments of clarity:
- Long walks (no phone/music)
- Hikes
- Showers

**Your brain needs space to process inputs and generate outputs.**
