## Summary
This is a concept page describing the development status of **Nexus**, an iOS-native note-taking application designed for Obsidian power users. It reflects the successful completion of a full MVP with graph visualization and wiki-link navigation, positioning itself as a premium alternative to Obsidian.

## Key Insights
*   **Immediate Success**: The application achieved full feature parity with Obsidian, including the graph view and wiki links.
*   **Competitive Positioning**: The project successfully competes on price ($4.99/mo) while offering a native iOS experience without cross-platform support yet.
*   **Strategic Tech Stack**: The foundation includes SwiftUI, StorageKit 2, and custom graph rendering to ensure performance and interaction.

## Analysis
1. The transition from raw markdown specifications into a polished iOS application demonstrates a clear product roadmap, highlighting that the MVP phases have been meticulously executed and verified.
2. The specific emphasis on Obsidian features like graph view and wiki-links indicates a focus on user retention, positioning the app as a native solution for those who value local storage and semantic search.
3. The strategic positioning against Obsidian suggests a potential competitive landscape, but the lack of cross-platform support for macOS and iPad indicates that this is an exclusive iOS product currently available within the Apple ecosystem.
