# Andrej Karpathy

> AI researcher, educator, and entrepreneur. Former Director of AI at Tesla Autopilot, founding member of OpenAI, and Stanford PhD under Fei-Fei Li. Known for makecourse-style deep learning education and pioneering work in AI agentic workflows.

## Background & Education

### Academic Foundation

Andrej Karpathy completed his **PhD at Stanford University** under the supervision of **Fei-Fei Li**, a pioneer in computer vision. His doctoral research focused on **convolutional and recurrent neural networks** for visual recognition tasks, particularly image captioning — generating natural language descriptions of images.

His academic work includes:
- **Deep Fragment Embeddings for Bidirectional Image-Sentence Mapping** (NIPS 2014)
- **neuraltalk** and **neuraltalk2**: Early image captioning systems using Torch/Lua
- **ConvNetJS**: Deep learning library implemented entirely in JavaScript for browser-based neural networks

### Industry Roles

#### OpenAI (Founding Member)

Karpathy was among the early researchers at **OpenAI**, the AI safety research company founded in 2015. At OpenAI, he worked on deep learning research and contributed to the organization's early research culture and direction before leaving to join Tesla.

#### Tesla (Director of AI, Autopilot)

From 2017 to 2022, Karpathy served as **Director of AI at Tesla**, leading the computer vision team for **Tesla Autopilot** — the company's advanced driver assistance system. He briefly also worked on **Tesla Optimus**, the company's humanoid robot project.

Key contributions at Tesla:
- Built and led the team responsible for Autopilot's visual perception system
- Developed the architecture for multi-task learning on Tesla's neural networks
- Presented at CVPR and other conferences on large-scale deep learning for automotive
- Introduced the "HydraNet" architecture for multi-task visual perception

He left Tesla in July 2022 and was not replaced directly; the Autopilot/AI team was reorganized.

## Educational Contributions

### Stanford CS231n

Karpathy was the **head TA for CS231n** (Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition) at Stanford, one of the most influential deep learning courses. His lecture notes and explanations became the de facto learning resource for computer vision with deep learning.

### DeepLearning.AI

He taught **Deep Learning Specialization** on Coursera (DeepLearning.AI), which has enrolled millions of students. The five-course sequence covers:
1. Neural Networks and Deep Learning
2. Improving Deep Neural Networks
3. Structuring Machine Learning Projects
4. Convolutional Neural Networks
5. Sequence Models

### Eureka Labs (Current Venture)

In 2024, Karpathy announced **Eureka Labs**, an AI-native education company:
> "Building a new kind of school that is AI-native — where the best human teachers and AI software collaborate to teach students."

The company aims to create AI teaching assistants that can adapt to individual student needs while maintaining the warmth and expertise of human educators.

## Research & Projects

### char-rnn (Character-Level Language Models)

One of Karpathy's most influential open-source projects. **char-rnn** demonstrated that recurrent neural networks could generate text character-by-character with surprising coherence:

```python
# Simplified char-rnn generation example
# Train on Shakespeare, generate Shakespeare-like text
# Train on code, generate code-like text
# Train on Linux source, generate compilable C code
```

This project popularized the idea of using RNNs/LSTMs for generative tasks and influenced the entire language model field.

### The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Recurrent Neural Networks

Karpathy's 2015 blog post with this title became seminal reading for anyone entering deep learning. It showed concrete examples of RNNs generating:
- Shakespearean text
- Wikipedia articles
- Computer code
- Mathematical papers

### Minimal RNN

Published a minimal, readable implementation of RNN training, stripping out all the complexity to reveal the core mechanics of how RNNs learn.

### Software 2.0

In 2017, Karpathy coined the concept of **"Software 2.0"** — the idea that machine learning would increasingly replace traditional symbolic programming:

> "The "software 2.0" stack is taking over in many areas where we previously used explicit algorithms, but it has a very different failure mode."

He distinguished between:
- **Software 1.0**: Explicit code written by humans
- **Software 2.0**: Code written by optimization (neural networks) based on a loss function and data

This conceptual framework has been widely cited and shaped how developers think about AI-integrated software.

## AI Agent Views & Recent Work

### LLMs and Agentic Workflows

Karpathy has been vocal about the trajectory of AI agents:

**On the current state**: He describes current LLMs as "fuzzy cognitive maps" — probabilistic systems that store knowledge in context rather than weights. They can retrieve and apply knowledge but reasoning is emergent rather than symbolic.

**On agents**: He's explored autonomous AI research agents that can run thousands of experiments. In 2024, he noted that his autonomous research agent ran **700 experiments in 2 days**, demonstrating the potential for AI-driven scientific discovery.

### The "No Priors" Podcast

Karpathy co-hosts the **"No Priors"** podcast with Sarah Guo, covering AI research, startups, and the future of AI. Guests include leading AI researchers, founders, and practitioners.

### "State of GPT" Presentations

His "State of GPT" presentations at Microsoft Build and other venues are widely watched resources explaining:
- How GPT-style language models are trained
- Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)
- Practical guidance for developers using LLMs
