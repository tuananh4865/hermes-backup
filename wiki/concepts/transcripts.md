# Transcripts

## Overview

Transcripts are textual representations of spoken conversations, meetings, or audio/video recordings. They convert speech into written text, preserving the exact words, speaker identification, and often timestamps for reference. Transcription technology has evolved significantly with the advent of automated speech recognition (ASR), particularly through models like [[Whisper]], which provide accurate, open-source transcription capabilities. Transcripts serve as the foundation for various downstream workflows including [[meeting notes]] generation, searchable archives, and content accessibility features.

## Use Cases

**Meeting Notes and Documentation**

Transcripts transform verbal discussions into searchable, shareable documents. Teams use transcripts to capture decisions, action items, and key discussion points without requiring manual note-taking. This ensures no critical information is lost and provides a reference trail for future meetings.

**Content Repurposing**

Written transcripts enable content creators to transform podcasts, interviews, and video content into blog posts, articles, social media snippets, and email newsletters. This extends the reach of audio and video content to audiences who prefer reading or need text-based access.

**Accessibility**

Transcripts make audio and video content accessible to deaf or hard-of-hearing individuals, non-native speakers, and anyone in sound-sensitive environments. Captions and subtitles derived from transcripts improve comprehension and compliance with accessibility regulations.

**Search and Reference**

Transcripts create searchable text archives of spoken content. This allows users to quickly locate specific information within long recordings, extract quotes, or build knowledge bases from previously ephemeral conversations.

## Tools

**Whisper** is the most widely adopted open-source transcription model, developed by OpenAI. It supports multiple languages, handles varying audio quality, and can run locally for privacy-sensitive applications. Alternatives include AssemblyAI, Rev, and built-in OS transcription services like Apple's Voice Memos or Google's speech recognition API.

For meeting-specific workflows, tools like Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, and Loop AI provide real-time transcription integrated with calendar systems, summarization, and collaboration features.
