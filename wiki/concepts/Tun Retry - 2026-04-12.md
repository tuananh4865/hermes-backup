# Tun Retry - 2026-04-12

## Summary
This transcript details the progress and strategy for upgrading a knowledge base (wiki) to meet a minimum quality score of 7.5/10. The process involves structured batch processing of content stubs, parallel delegation tasks, and careful monitoring of file changes. Key findings indicate that previous self-healing agents had already performed significant expansion work, shifting the focus from simple structural scoring to actual content quality improvement.

## Key Insights
- **Quality Metric Discrepancy**: The current quality scoring system (max ~5.5 structurally) does not accurately reflect the actual content quality; high-quality pages often score lower than expected.
- **Pre-existing Content Expansion**: Many files processed in later batches were already expanded by previous subagents, meaning git diffs only show truly new or updated files, confirming prior work was successful.
- **Stub Expansion Goal**: The primary objective is to expand all remaining content stubs from 16-17 words up to 300+ words to achieve the target quality threshold.

## Analysis
The project utilized a structured batch processing approach across ten major phases (Batch 1 through Batch 10), successfully processing approximately 190 pages of content. The delegation strategy involved parallel `delegate_task` calls, maximizing speed while maintaining checkpoint tracking for interruption resilience. A significant milestone was Batch 9, which resulted in the creation of 244 new files, demonstrating the powerful effect of subagents in generating related concept files (e.g., cryptography concepts) during expansion.

A critical decision was made to prioritize content quality over structural scoring. The analysis confirms that while the system can structurally score pages highly, the true measure of success lies in achieving substantial word counts and substantive content depth. This required adjusting the target from a high structural score to ensuring 300-500 words for expanded stubs.

The primary ongoing challenge is the continuous creation of new, small stubs by the self-healing system. Once the main batches are complete, the system auto-generates numerous new pages, requiring continued maintenance and handling of these orphan files to ensure the knowledge base remains comprehensive and high quality.
