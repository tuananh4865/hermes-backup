# Wiki Quality Campaign

## Overview

The Wiki Quality Campaign is a systematic initiative to elevate wiki pages from poor or fair quality to high-quality standards through structured improvement processes. In knowledge management ecosystems, quality disparities create fragmented, unreliable, or incomplete information bases that undermine the utility of the wiki as a reference system. A quality campaign addresses this by establishing clear standards, identifying gaps, and methodically upgrading content to meet those benchmarks.

The campaign draws from broader [[knowledge-quality-system]] principles and [[information-quality]] frameworks, adapting them to the specific context of wiki-based documentation. The goal is not merely cosmetic improvement but ensuring that every page provides accurate, comprehensive, well-organized, and actionable information that serves its intended audience effectively.

## Key Concepts

### Quality Dimensions

Wiki quality is multidimensional, encompassing several key aspects:

- **Accuracy**: Information must be correct, verifiable, and free from factual errors or outdated content
- **Completeness**: Pages should cover their subject matter comprehensively without significant gaps
- **Clarity**: Writing should be clear, well-structured, and accessible to the target audience
- **Actionability**: Readers should be able to apply the knowledge gained from a page
- **Consistency**: Content should align with other wiki pages and use consistent terminology

### Quality Tiers

Content quality typically exists on a spectrum:

| Tier | Description | Characteristics |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Stub | Minimal content | Few sentences, missing key information |
| Developing | Partial coverage | Covers basics but lacks depth |
| Fair | Adequate coverage | Core content present but may lack polish |
| Good | Solid coverage | Well-written, mostly complete |
| Comprehensive | Complete coverage | Thorough, authoritative, exemplary |

### Quality Scoring

The [[knowledge-quality-system]] provides quantitative scoring mechanisms that evaluate pages across multiple dimensions. Scores typically aggregate factors like word count (as a proxy for depth), structural elements present (headings, code blocks, wikilinks), and content organization.

## How It Works

A Wiki Quality Campaign typically follows a structured workflow:

1. **Audit Phase**: Survey all wiki pages to establish baseline quality scores
2. **Prioritization**: Identify high-value pages that would benefit most from improvement
3. **Standard Setting**: Define style guides, templates, and quality criteria
4. **Improvement Cycle**: Systematically upgrade pages, focusing on structural and content enhancements
5. **Verification**: Confirm that upgraded pages meet quality thresholds
6. **Maintenance**: Establish ongoing quality monitoring to prevent degradation

The campaign may operate as a one-time initiative or as continuous improvement program, depending on organizational needs.

## Practical Applications

Wiki Quality Campaigns are particularly valuable in several contexts:

- **Engineering Teams**: Maintaining accurate technical documentation as systems evolve
- **Research Organizations**: Ensuring knowledge bases reflect current understanding
- **Product Documentation**: Keeping user-facing help content current and usable
- **Personal Knowledge Management**: Applying principles to individual PKM systems

## Examples

A typical improvement might transform a stub page:

```markdown
# API Gateway
API gateways manage API traffic.
```

Into a comprehensive article with multiple sections, code examples, and related concept links covering protocols, rate limiting, authentication, and monitoring.

## Personal Notes

Quality campaigns require sustained effort and clear ownership. It's easy to let documentation decay—establishing review cycles and quality gates helps maintain standards over time. Consider integrating quality metrics into content creation workflows rather than treating quality as a separate initiative.
