# Zettelkasten

Zettelkasten (German for "slip box") is a knowledge management method developed by sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who used it to write over 70 books and 400 articles.

## Core Principles

1. **Atomic Notes**: Each note captures one idea only
2. **Unique Identifiers**: Each note has an ID for permanent referencing
3. **Links Over Hierarchy**: Connections matter more than folders
4. **Your Own Words**: Reformulate ideas in your understanding
5. **Emergence**: The graph of notes generates new insights

## Key Concepts

- **Fleeting Notes**: Quick captures to process later
- **Literature Notes**: Summaries of source material
- **Permanent Notes**: Atomic ideas in your own words
- **Index**: Entry points into the note graph

## Tools

- **Analog**: Luhmann's physical slip boxes (paper-based)
- **Digital**: Obsidian, LogSeq, Roam Research, Notion
