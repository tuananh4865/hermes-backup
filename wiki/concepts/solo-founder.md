# Solo Founder

## Overview

A solo founder is an entrepreneur who builds and runs a company without co-founders. In 2026, AI tools enable solo founders to build companies that previously required entire teams.

## Why Solo in 2026?

### AI as Force Multiplier
- **Coding:** AI coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor) write 10x more code
- **Writing:** AI for documentation, marketing, customer communication
- **Operations:** AI agents handle support, sales, admin
- **Strategy:** AI provides expertise previously requiring advisors

### Benefits of Solo

1. **Speed:** No consensus building, faster decisions
2. **Alignment:** 100% ownership of vision
3. **Simplicity:** Fewer moving parts, less coordination
4. **Economics:** Full equity upside

### Challenges

1. **Loneliness:** No co-founder for brainstorming
2. **Skill Gaps:** Must learn multiple domains
3. **Capacity:** Time is the ultimate constraint
4. **Funding:** Some VCs prefer co-founders

## How to Succeed as Solo Founder

### Phase 1: Validate
1. Identify pain point
2. Ship MVP with AI tools
3. Get first paying customers
4. Validate demand

### Phase 2: Build
1. Use AI coding agents for product
2. Automate operations with n8n/Zapier
3. Handle support with AI chatbots
4. Use AI for marketing content

### Phase 3: Scale
1. Add human help for bottlenecks
2. Build team for things AI can't do
3. Raise funding if needed
4. Maintain culture and vision

## Mental Models

### Ship First, Perfect Later
AI enables rapid iteration. Ship v1, get feedback, improve.

### Cash Flow > Valuation
Solo founders need to be profitable or funded. Focus on revenue.

### Automate Everything Possible
Every hour saved is an hour invested in growth.

### Network Intentionally
Solo doesn't mean isolated. Build relationships with other founders.

## When to Add Co-Founders

Consider bringing on co-founders when:
- Fundraising requires it (some VCs demand it)
- You need specific expertise (CTO, sales)
- Workload is unsustainable solo
- You find someone who shares your vision
