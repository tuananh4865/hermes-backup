# Seo

## Overview

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of improving a website's visibility in organic (non-paid) search engine results. Search engines like Google use complex algorithms to crawl, index, and rank web pages based on hundreds of factors. SEO aims to align your website with these ranking criteria, making it easier for your target audience to discover your content.

Unlike paid search advertising (PPC), SEO focuses on earning visibility through merit rather than purchasing ad space. A well-executed SEO strategy builds sustainable organic traffic that grows as your site gains authority, often providing higher ROI over time compared to paid channels.

## On-Page Factors

On-page SEO encompasses optimizations made directly within your website that directly affect rankings.

**Content Quality** is the most critical factor. Search engines prioritize content demonstrating expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness (E-E-A-T). Content should comprehensively answer user questions and keep visitors engaged.

**Keyword Optimization** involves strategically placing relevant terms in titles, headings, meta descriptions, and body text. Modern SEO favors natural, contextually relevant language over forced keyword repetition.

**Header Tags (H1-H6)** structure content hierarchically, helping users and search engines navigate your page. Proper heading structure signals the organization of your content.

**Internal Linking** connects your pages together, distributing link equity and helping search bots discover new content while improving user navigation.

## Off-Page Factors

Off-page SEO involves activities outside your website that influence rankings, with backlinks being the primary factor.

**Backlinks** act as votes of confidence from other sites. When authoritative websites link to your content, search engines interpret this as a signal of value and trustworthiness. Links from reputable, relevant sites carry significantly more weight than low-quality backlinks.

**Domain Authority**, a metric developed by Moz, predicts how well a website will rank based on its backlink profile. While not a direct Google ranking factor, it provides a useful benchmark for competitive positioning.

**Social Signals** such as shares and engagement may indirectly influence SEO by increasing content visibility and the likelihood of earning backlinks.

## Technical SEO

Technical SEO ensures search engines can efficiently crawl, index, and render your website.

**Site Speed** affects both user experience and rankings. Google uses Core Web Vitals—measuring loading performance, interactivity, and visual stability—as ranking signals.

**Mobile-Friendliness** is essential since Google uses mobile-first indexing, meaning the mobile version of your site determines rankings.

**XML Sitemaps** help search engines discover and index pages. **Robots.txt** controls which pages bots can access.

**HTTPS/SSL Encryption** provides security and is a confirmed ranking factor. **Structured Data (Schema Markup)** helps search engines understand content context and can enable rich snippets.

## AI's Impact on SEO

AI is fundamentally transforming SEO practices. Google's algorithms like BERT and RankBrain understand search intent with unprecedented sophistication, interpreting natural language queries and assessing content quality beyond keywords.

Generative AI in search (such as Google AI Overviews) now provides direct answers at the top of results, shifting some traffic away from traditional listings. This makes optimizing for featured snippets and demonstrating E-E-A-T increasingly important.

Search engines now actively detect and demote AI-generated spam while rewarding genuinely helpful content. The Search Generative Experience (SGE) represents a shift toward conversational, multi-turn search experiences requiring comprehensive content that answers complex questions.
